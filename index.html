Spelling Homophones and Other Training (SHOT)

Spelling Homophones and Other Training (SHOT) is a computer program designed to assist English language learners with homophones. English is a notoriously difficult language to learn, and homophones, homographs, and homonyms can be “very difficult to identify and to understand [for] learners of English as the second language” (page 275). The main focus of SHOT is homophones since they are easier to spot and check (it is much easier to check spelling than pronunciation or meaning). However, functionality for the other two could be implemented in the future.

SHOT is a highly customizable program. It allows learners to select only the words they want to work with and includes filters to sort through the list of words more easily. Additionally, the list of words contains examples and definitions that learners could use to study (currently, these are tooltips seen by hovering the mouse over the word in the list). Furthermore, the save and load feature allows people to share customized lessons or return to the same lesson later. Teachers could use saving and loading to give each student the same lesson relevant to the current course material. In the future, this could be expanded to include custom words so that lesson plans can be even more personalized and focused on what is important to the teacher or individual learners. Additionally, SHOT includes an optional self-test to check and rate learners’ understanding of homophones. This test allows SHOT to individualize a lesson plan focusing on homophones that present difficulty to a specific learner.

SHOT also has methods for learning as a group as well as individually. For example, a group of learners could select the same lesson plan and compete against each other for the highest score. In the future, a leaderboard could be implemented to compare scores against other learners. Alternatively, individual learners could use the same lesson plan and compete against themselves for the quickest time or highest score. Learners can limit a lesson by time or the number of questions asked. When the lesson is complete, learners will see statistics on their performance, including performance for specific words and overall performance. Additionally, there is an option to hide the timer, as it may be stressful for some learners to see time ticking away; time will still be tracked for the performance statistics.

In the future, SHOT could be expanded to include homonyms and homographs. This expansion could mean including speech recognition for homograph training, as it would allow for more types of problems. For example, a sentence could be displayed to the learner for them to speak. Alternatively, there could be a matching task where learners are played the sound of the word which they then match to the correct meaning. On the other hand, homonym training may be limited since there are few ways to check if learners understand the meaning of two words that are spelled and pronounced the same. One way to check understanding of homographs could be to have a matching task where one side is a sentence and the other is the word’s definition.

DISCLAIMER: SHOT in its current state does not contain many of the features presented above. This is simply a description of the intention behind the program.
